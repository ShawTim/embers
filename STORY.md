# Embers of Aetheria — Story Bible

## World

**Aetheria** — a realm lit by the Ember Throne, a sacred flame that has burned for a thousand years in the heart of the capital Valdris. The Throne's light repels the Umbral Tide, an ancient darkness that once swallowed half the world. So long as the Throne burns, Aetheria endures.

But the flame is dimming.

## Characters

### Player Party

**Kael Ashwood** (Lord) — Young lord of House Ashwood, a border province. Raised on tales of the Ember Throne's glory, he has never known war until the night his estate burns. Determined, compassionate, carries the weight of leadership he never asked for.

**Lyra Sunweave** (Cleric) — A healer from the Temple of Embers in Valdris. Sent to Ashwood on a routine pilgrimage, she arrives the night of the attack. She harbors a secret: she can sense the Ember Throne's light fading, though she dare not speak it aloud.

**Borin Steadwall** (Knight) — A veteran who served Kael's father. His shield has guarded three generations of Ashwoods. He knows the old wars, the old tactics, and the old costs. He fights not for glory but because he cannot bear to see another home burn.

**Serra Vale** (Archer) — A hunter from the northern forests. Sharp-eyed, sharp-tongued. She was tracking the bandits who raided her village when she stumbled into Kael's battle. She joins for revenge, but stays for something she won't name.

### Later Recruits (Ch3-15)

**Maren Blackthorn** (Mage) — A scholar of the Arcane Academy who discovered the Umbral Cult's activities. Joins in Ch3 at the Forsaken Shrine.

**Darius Ironheart** (Cavalier) — A deserting knight from the Valdris city guard. Knows the city's defenses. Joins in Ch6.

**Yuki Frostwing** (Pegasus Knight) — A pegasus rider from the mountain clans. Joins in Ch13.

### Antagonists

**Garrick the Cruel** (Ch1-2 Boss) — Bandit leader, but merely a pawn. He wears the Umbral sigil unknowingly.

**Acolyte Veyne** (Ch3-5) — A mid-ranking Umbral Cultist who orchestrates the corruption of the Shrine of Embers.

**Inquisitor Malachar** (Ch6-10) — The Cult's military commander. A fallen paladin who once guarded the Ember Throne. He believes the Throne must be extinguished for humanity to be free.

**Lord Zethar, the Endless Night** (Ch16-20, Final Boss) — The immortal leader of the Umbral Cult. He was once the Throne's first guardian, who turned against it when he saw that the flame's power was borrowed from a dying god. He seeks to free the god by extinguishing the Throne — even if it means the world drowns in darkness.

## Chapter Outline (20 Chapters)

### Act I: The Gathering Shadow (Ch1-5)

**Ch1 — Embers in the Night**
Kael's estate is attacked by bandits. He meets Lyra and Borin in the defense. Garrick leads the raid. After defeating him, they find the Umbral sigil burned into his wrist — a mark of the Cult of Endless Night, thought to be a children's tale.

**Ch2 — The Forest of Whispers**
Pursuing fleeing bandits into the ancient forest. Serra joins. They encounter Umbral Acolytes for the first time — cultists channeling corrupted fire magic. The forest itself is sick, its roots darkened.

**Ch3 — The Forsaken Shrine**
Deep in the forest lies the Shrine of Embers, a secondary flame-site that once bolstered the Throne's power. It has been corrupted. Maren joins here. Boss: Acolyte Veyne, who flees after defeat, warning that "the Lord of Night has already begun."

**Ch4 — Crossroads of Fate**
Ambush at a crossroads. The party must hold for 8 turns against waves of cultists while refugees flee. A mysterious hooded figure watches from a distance — Darius, who does not yet reveal himself.

**Ch5 — Gates of Valdris**
The party reaches the capital to warn the Council. The gates are besieged by Cult forces. After breaking through, they find the Council skeptical — the Ember Throne still burns, they say. All is well. All is well.

### Act II: The City of Light (Ch6-10)

**Ch6 — A City Divided**
Urban combat in Valdris's streets. Cultists have infiltrated the city. Darius joins, revealing the Cult has sympathizers inside the Council. Boss: A Cult captain.

**Ch7 — The Undercity**
Navigating the sewers beneath Valdris to find the Cult's hidden temple. Fog of war. They discover the Cult is siphoning power from the Ember Throne through underground channels.

**Ch8 — The Arena of Kings**
The Council convenes to debate. Cultists attack the Council Hall. The party must protect the Council members for 10 turns. Inquisitor Malachar reveals himself — he is a former Throne Guardian.

**Ch9 — Betrayal at Dawn**
A Council member is revealed as a Cult sympathizer. City guards turn on the party. Moral choice: spare or execute the traitor (affects later dialogue). Boss: The traitor's bodyguard.

**Ch10 — The Fall of Valdris**
The Cult launches a full assault. The party must defend the Ember Throne chamber. Even in victory, the Throne flickers. Malachar escapes, taking a shard of the Ember with him. The party must flee the burning city.

### Act III: The Wild Marches (Ch11-15)

**Ch11 — Into the Mountains**
The party retreats into the Frostpeak Mountains. Harsh terrain, limited supplies. They meet mountain clan scouts who are suspicious of lowlanders.

**Ch12 — The Dragon's Roost**
A corrupted dragon nests in the mountain pass — infused with Umbral energy. Boss battle. Yuki's clan witnesses the party's valor and offers alliance.

**Ch13 — The Pegasus Riders**
Yuki joins formally. Aerial enemies (Cult wyvern riders). The party learns that Malachar has established a fortress in the old borderlands — the Umbral Fortress.

**Ch14 — The Frozen Lake**
Crossing a vast frozen lake. Unique ice terrain. The ice cracks under heavy units. Ambush from beneath.

**Ch15 — The Umbral Fortress**
Infiltrating the Fortress. Multiple objectives in sequence — breach the gate, secure the courtyard, confront Malachar. Boss: Inquisitor Malachar. In his dying breath, he reveals the truth about Zethar — that the first Guardian became the ultimate enemy.

### Act IV: The Endless Night (Ch16-20)

**Ch16 — The Void Gate**
The party races to close a portal that Zethar has opened — a direct channel to the Umbral realm. Reinforcements every turn. The Void Gate pulses with dark energy.

**Ch17 — Echoes of the Past**
Flashback chapter. The player controls Kael's father, Lord Ashwood the Elder, 20 years ago. He fought alongside a young Malachar and a mysterious figure — Zethar, before his fall. This chapter reveals the origin of the conflict.

**Ch18 — The Lord of Night**
Confrontation with Zethar at the Throne's foundation. He offers Kael a choice: join him in extinguishing the Throne to free the dying god, or fight to maintain a flame that he claims is a lie.

**Ch19 — The Last Ember**
The party must defend the rekindled Ember for 15 turns while Lyra performs a ritual to restore the Throne's full power. Waves of Umbral creatures. If Lyra falls, all is lost.

**Ch20 — Dawn of Aetheria**
Final battle with Zethar in his true form. Multiple phases. The fate of the Ember Throne — and the world — is decided. Victory brings dawn to Aetheria for the first time in a thousand years.

## Dialogue System Design

### Trigger Types
1. **Pre-battle cutscene** — story text + character portraits, shown before each chapter
2. **Boss encounter** — triggered when boss is first attacked or reaches low HP
3. **Boss death** — dying words, reveals plot
4. **Character interaction** — specific units adjacent to each other trigger conversation
5. **Chapter victory** — post-battle reflection + setup for next chapter
6. **Mid-chapter events** — triggered by turn count or unit reaching a specific tile

### Dialogue Format
Each dialogue line:
- Speaker (unit ID or "narrator")
- Text (EN + ZH)
- Portrait mood (neutral, angry, sad, determined)
- Optional: camera focus on a 3D position
